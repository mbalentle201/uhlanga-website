ABOUT UHLANGA BOOKS.

"We believe telling the story to each & everyone in South Africa. Authentically, genuinely, & without watering down or filtering the author." - By Zimkhitha Zilo

Uhlanga books was established in 2019. We specialise in self-publishing, so far we have managed to publish 9 books, all of which are astonishing in quality and content.

We publish all genres from:

  • Poetry
  • Fiction
  • Non-fiction
  • Short-Stories
  • Adult erotic-fiction
  • Romance
  • Study Guides

Zimkhitha Zilo - The Founder

"I got introduced to reading at a very early age, and i guess the universe knew that i would need a place to hide and disappear to at some point in my life"

i feel like reading made me more aware as a person, reading also boosted my confidence and helped me become a "encyclopedia" random facts. When i moved to Cape Town from the Free-state, i was located in khayelitsha and what i experienced after that was just kids loitering in the streets and it just being flooded with alcohol bottles and cigarette stubs, That right there for me was a culture shock. I would just close my and envision the whole streets flooded with books children reading instead of indulging in substance abuse.

Diary of A Liberal Black Woman

This is a Fiction novel,set in East London. Pule,is young, happy, about to join one of the biggest firms in the country and earn lots of money. As perfect as all that was, there was one thing missing, a certain accessory every woman craves......"The man of her dreams" Sakhi comes into her life, like a breath of fresh air, and ticks all Pule's boxes.....Could he be the one? Finding uLoyiso eLokshin. "Finding Loyiso in the township"

A coming of age story about growing up in Khayelitsha at the height of apartheid. Loyiso recounts a happy childhood, despite his fathers drinking and his mothers intermittent hospitalization for an unnamed illness. He was born in 1978, to parents that were both teachers. He grows up in a tight-knit family surrounded by extended family and the neighbours that become his surrogate family when his parents separate, and later on when his mother goes to study further leaving him and his siblings with their grandparents. He writes about his primary school years and the enduring friendships; his high school years where he discovered his love for sports.

Kilombo "a dream that lived"

From the cradle to the grave, Blacks live to escape death. A trial happened many years ago in lands far away. The history of those who are greedy has judged your black skin harshly, our collective death serves their collective belly. Many die in infacy and are only made mention of in stats of infant mortality rates by academics. Those who survive infant mortality, wait their day in a prison cell with no prospects of clemency or parole. The guillotine falls indiscriminately, chopping the necks of both the young and the old. If HIV and AIDS missed you, you have violent crime waiting for you in the next corner. If you escape, black on black violence, motivated by politics of the stamoch may end your miserable life.

Read the Book
Being Black

"The captivating narrative of Theo Mayekiso's journey from Ngcobo in the Eastern Cape to Worcester in the Boland provides clear and mind shifting perspectives that are necessary to understand the complexity of interracial relationships in South Africa. It is a story of an authentic quest for true reconciliation ... He left the rural life of the Eastern Cape without expecting how his deepest understanding of ubuntu (umntu ngumntu ngabantu), meaning I am because we are ... would be challenged." from the foreword by Nelis Janse van Rensburg, Moderator of the Dutch Reformed Church in South Africa.
